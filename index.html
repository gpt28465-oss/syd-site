Al-Imam Al-Sayyid Musa Al-Sadr

Scholar, reformer, and voice for dignity and social justice in Lebanon.

Discover his life View the timeline
  • Born: 1928 – Qom, Iran
  • Role: Head of the Supreme Islamic Shiite Council in Lebanon (1969)
  • Founded: The Amal Movement (1974)
  • Disappeared: August 31, 1978 – Libya

About

Sayyid Musa al-Sadr (1928–unknown) was a Lebanese Shia cleric and public intellectual. He moved to Lebanon in 1959, quickly becoming a leading voice for national unity, education, and social development, especially in the historically marginalized south.

He spearheaded community institutions, advocated dialogue across religious traditions, and worked to strengthen the state’s role in providing dignity and opportunity for all citizens.

His unexplained disappearance in Libya on August 31, 1978, with Sheikh Muhammad Ya‘qub and journalist ‘Abbas Badreddine, remains a central unresolved case in Lebanon’s modern history.

Scholar & Reformer

Combined religious scholarship with civic action: education, healthcare, and social solidarity.

Bridge-Builder

Promoted dialogue among Lebanon’s communities and institutions to reinforce national unity.

Enduring Question

His fate remains unknown; the quest for truth continues as part of the public conscience.

Timeline

  1. 1928
    Born in Qom to a scholarly family rooted in Jabal ‘Amil.
  2. 1959
    Settles in Tyre, Lebanon; launches wide-ranging social and educational initiatives.
  3. 1969
    Elected head of the Supreme Islamic Shiite Council in Lebanon.
  4. 1974
    Helps found the Amal Movement amid growing security and social challenges.
  5. Aug 1978
    Travels to Libya; disappears with Sheikh Muhammad Ya‘qub and journalist ‘Abbas Badreddine.

Legacy

  • A model of faith engaged with public life: dignity, education, and social justice.
  • Institutions and movements that continue to shape civic participation.
  • An enduring call for dialogue and responsible citizenship.
